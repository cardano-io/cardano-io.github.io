Ouroboros

Ouroboros is a new proof of stake algorithm used by the Cardano protocol.

Why have we created Ouroboros?

  • The algorithm is a crucial part of the infrastructure that supports the Ada cryptocurrency and is a major innovation in blockchain technology.
  • Ouroboros eliminates the need for an energy-hungry proof of work protocol, which stands as a barrier to blockchain scaling up for much wider use.
  • It’s a provably secure system comparable to that of Bitcoin’s blockchain, which has never been compromised.
  • It’s peer reviewed by scientists, academics and engineers to make sure that it lorem ipsum doler emit. Our paper ‘Ouroboros: A Provably Secure Proof-of-Stake Blockchain Protocol’ was accepted at Crypto 2017, the leading cryptography conference.

What is a proof of stake algorithm?

Cardano uses a new proof of stake algorithm called Ouroboros to determine how individual nodes reach consensus about the network.

In proof of work, miners invest computing power to compete to be chosen as the leader who gets to make the next block and win a reward for doing so. By contrast, in proof of stake, the stakeholder who will form the next block is randomly selected, proportionally to the size of the stake that they have, according to the blockchain ledger.

Why is it so different from what existed before?

In proof of work, miners invest computing power to compete to be chosen as the leader who gets to make the next block and win a reward for doing so.

By contrast, in proof of stake, the stakeholder who will form the next block is randomly selected, proportionally to the size of the stake that they have, according to the blockchain ledger.

There have been a number of attempts by other cryptocurrencies to develop a proof of stake algorithm, although these protocols have suffered from flaws and have not been shown to be provably secure.

Who is behind this?

Ouroboros was designed a team led by IOHK Chief Scientist, Professor Aggelos Kiayias.
